Jollof Rice

Description

A recipe for cooking Jollof rice, West Africa/'s most popular dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 3 to 4 fresh tomatoes (or 1 can of chopped tomatoes)
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 red bell pepper (blended)
  • 1 scotch bonnet or chili pepper (optional for heat)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (minced)
  • 2 cups chicken stock or water
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 stock cube (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Optional: mixed veggies or cooked meat (chicken, beef)

Steps

  1. Blend the sauce: Blend tomatoes, bell pepper, scotch bonnet, onion (half of it), and garlic into a smooth mix.
  2. Make the base: In a large pot, heat oil. Sauté the chopped onion until soft. Add tomato paste and fry for a few minutes.
  3. Cook the sauce: Pour in the blended mix. Cook on medium heat for about 10–15 minutes until it reduces and the oil begins to separate.
  4. Season it: Add thyme, curry, bay leaves, ginger, salt, and stock cube. Stir well.
  5. Add rice & liquid: Add the rice and stir until well coated. Then pour in the stock (just enough to cover the rice). Reduce heat to low.
  6. Simmer: Cover with foil and a lid. Let it cook slowly for 30 to 40 minutes. Stir occasionally and add a splash of water if it dries out too soon.
  7. Fluff and serve: Once the rice is cooked and has soaked up all the flavors, fluff with a fork. Serve hot!
